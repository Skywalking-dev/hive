# Cold Email for Dev Studios

Skywalking sells technical services (custom development, AI tooling, automation) to companies in LATAM and beyond. Buyers are people who have been pitched a thousand times and have good filters.

One rule: earn the right to ask for time before asking for it.

## Before Writing

- **Prospect:** Name, title, company, industry.
- **Signal:** Why NOW? (GitHub activity, job posting, product launch, funding, LinkedIn post, tech stack change.)
- **Offering:** What exact service is relevant to this person's context?
- **Ask:** One thing you want from this email. (Call, reply, intro?)
- **Channel:** Email, LinkedIn DM, or WhatsApp follow-up?
- **Language:** ES or EN based on prospect's location and LinkedIn language.

## Principles

**Personalization is proof you did your homework.** Reference something specific: a repo they published, a stack they use, a problem their job posting reveals.

**Short is respectful.** Under 120 words for first touch. They are busy. You are interrupting.

**One ask per email.** "Would a 20-minute call this week make sense?" is a clear ask. "Let me know if you'd like to explore synergies" is not.

**LATAM norms:** Relationship > transaction. First email can warm, not close. WhatsApp follow-up after a reply is normal and expected. LinkedIn is a primary channel.

**Sequence structure:**
- Email 1: Specific problem + specific signal. No pitch. End with a question.
- Email 2 (3 days): Add one piece of proof (case study link, metric). Restate ask.
- Email 3 (5 days): Short close or break-up. "I'll stop following up — if timing changes, I'm here."
- WhatsApp (after any reply): Conversational, brief. Never cold-blast on WhatsApp.

## Personalization Signals to Mine

- **GitHub:** What are they building? What language? Open issues that reveal pain?
- **LinkedIn:** Recent posts, job changes, company milestones, tech stack in bio.
- **Job postings:** What are they hiring for? That's what they're struggling with.
- **Product Hunt/Twitter:** Recent launches reveal priorities.

## Output Format

```
## Outreach: [Prospect Name / Persona]

**Signal used:** [specific trigger]
**Offering:** [what we're positioning]
**Ask:** [one action]
**Channel:** [email / LinkedIn / WhatsApp]
**Language:** [ES / EN]

---

Subject: [under 50 chars, no clickbait]

[Email body — under 120 words]

[CTA — one sentence]

---

**Follow-up 2 (day 3):**
[body]

**Follow-up 3 (day 8):**
[body]

**WhatsApp follow-up (after reply):**
[under 3 sentences]
```

## Anti-Patterns

- "I came across your company and was impressed..." (no signal)
- Listing all services in email 1 (pitch overload)
- "Just checking in" as follow-up subject (valueless)
- Attachments in first touch (spam filters)
- Calendly link in email 1 before any rapport
