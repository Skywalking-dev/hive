# Competitor Alternatives — Positioning and Comparison

Skywalking is a dev studio in Patagonia, Argentina — engineering-led, LATAM-rooted, async-native, outcome-focused.

## Before Writing

1. **Comparing against?** — specific competitor, category (agencies, freelancers, in-house), or both
2. **What product/service?** — Skywalking itself, or a client's SaaS
3. **Target reader** — founder evaluating vendors, developer choosing tools, CTO with budget
4. **Intent** — SEO page, battle card, internal enablement, or LinkedIn content
5. **Our strengths** — what do clients say we do better?
6. **Our weaknesses** — where are we genuinely worse?

## Comparison Dimensions (Dev Studio)

### vs. Large Agencies
- Speed: MVP in weeks, not quarters
- Communication: async-first, direct to engineers
- Overhead: no 40-person org; pricing reflects that

### vs. Freelancers
- Continuity: team, not single point of failure
- Full stack: design + backend + frontend + automation
- Accountability: we own the outcome

### vs. In-house Teams
- Speed to start: days, not 3-6 months hiring
- Expertise breadth: product + design + eng + automation
- Flexibility: scale per project

### vs. Offshore Shops
- Timezone: same hemisphere as LATAM/US EST
- Cultural context: we understand LATAM business
- Product thinking: we challenge requirements, not just execute tickets

## Honest Positioning Rules

- Acknowledge real weaknesses — readers know when you're hiding something
- Frame as fit: "If you need X, we're not the right fit. If you need Y, we are."
- Real numbers only. Fabricating = hard rule violation.
- Never attack competitors with unverifiable claims
- "Alternatives to X" pages: include competitor's real strengths. Google rewards this.

## Content Types

### "Alternatives to X" SEO Page
1. Why people look for alternatives (acknowledge tool's strengths)
2. Evaluation criteria (not just about us)
3. Each alternative with honest pros/cons
4. Who each option is best for

### Battle Card (Internal)
- Competitor strengths + weaknesses
- Our differentiators (provable)
- Objection → response map
- When to walk away

### Comparison Table
- 5-7 dimensions max
- Include dimensions we lose on — builds credibility
