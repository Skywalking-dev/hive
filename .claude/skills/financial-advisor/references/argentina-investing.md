# Argentina Investing Reference

## CEDEARs — Complete Guide

### What Are CEDEARs?
Certificados de Deposito Argentinos — certificates representing fractions of foreign shares traded on BYMA in Argentine Pesos. Each CEDEAR has a ratio (e.g., 1:10 means 1 CEDEAR = 1/10 of foreign share).

### How Pricing Works
```
CEDEAR Price (ARS) = Foreign Stock Price (USD) * CCL Rate / Ratio
```

Example: Apple at $200, CCL at 1,200 ARS/USD, ratio 1:10
```
CEDEAR AAPL = $200 * 1,200 / 10 = ARS 24,000
```

## Obligaciones Negociables (Corporate Bonds)

### What Are They?
Debt instruments issued by private companies, denominated in USD or ARS. Pay periodic coupons + return principal at maturity.

### Types
- **Hard dollar**: Pay in USD (most desirable for Argentine investors)
- **Dollar-linked**: Pay in ARS adjusted by official USD exchange rate
- **CER-linked**: Pay in ARS adjusted by CPI (inflation protection)
- **Badlar**: Pay in ARS at interbank rate

### Key Considerations
- Typically yield 5-10% in USD
- Minimum investment varies ($100-$1,000 USD typically)
- Credit risk depends on issuing company
- Secondary market liquidity can be thin
- Tax: interest generally taxed; some exemptions apply

### Where to Buy
All Argentine brokers (IOL, Balanz, PPI, Bull Market, Cocos) offer ONs through BYMA.

## Fondos Comunes de Inversion (FCIs)

### Categories

| Type | Risk | Return Profile | Best For |
|------|------|---------------|----------|
| **Money Market** | Very Low | Below inflation but instant liquidity | Parking cash |
| **Money Market USD** | Low | Low yield in USD | USD parking |
| **Renta Fija** | Low-Medium | Bonds; stable returns | Conservative |
| **Renta Variable** | High | Stocks; volatile | Growth |
| **Renta Mixta** | Medium | Balanced stocks + bonds | Moderate |
| **Infraestructura** | Medium-High | Real estate, infrastructure | Alternatives |
| **CEDEAR** | Medium-High | Foreign equities via CEDEARs | International |

### Key Features
- Managed by SGFCIs (fund management companies)
- NAV calculated daily
- No minimum holding period for money market
- Low minimum investment (often ARS 1,000)
- Regulated by CNV
- Compare at cafci.org.ar

### Selection Criteria
1. TNA (Tasa Nominal Anual) vs inflation
2. Management fees (comision de gestion)
3. Fund size (larger = more liquid)
4. Track record (3-year returns)
5. Manager reputation

## Plazo Fijo Comparison

### Traditional vs UVA

| Feature | Traditional | UVA |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Minimum Term | 30 days | 90 days |
| Rate | Fixed TNA (~40-47%) | CPI + ~1% |
| Best When | TNA > inflation | Inflation rising/unpredictable |
| Risk | Inflation surprises | Locked for 90 days minimum |
| Flexibility | High | Low |
| Current Winner (Feb 2026) | Traditional (TNA > CPI) | For inflation protection |

### Decision Framework
- **Inflation trending down** → Traditional PF (lock in high TNA)
- **Inflation accelerating** → UVA (guaranteed real return)
- **Uncertainty** → Split 50/50

## Dollar MEP Mechanism

### How It Works
1. Buy a bond in ARS on the local market (e.g., AL30 or GD30)
2. Wait parking period (if applicable — eliminated for personas humanas in 2026)
3. Sell the same bond in USD (denominacion "D")
4. The implied exchange rate = ARS price / USD price = Dollar MEP
5. Receive USD in your brokerage account

### Typical Bonds for MEP
- **AL30/AL30D**: Most liquid, tightest spread
- **GD30/GD30D**: Alternative
- **Spread**: Usually 0.5-2% cost vs theoretical rate

### Key Rules (2026)
- Parking eliminated for personas humanas
- Enterprises still subject to parking and restrictions
- No limit on USD amount for individuals
- Phase 3: floating within BCRA bands

## Bimonetario Strategy

### Concept
Maintain assets in both ARS and USD to hedge against currency risk while taking advantage of ARS yield opportunities.

### Typical Split
- **50-70% in USD-linked assets**: CEDEARs, ONs hard dollar, crypto
- **30-50% in ARS assets**: Plazo fijo, FCIs, Merval stocks
- Adjust based on CCL trend and inflation expectations

### Key Principle
- When ARS yields >> inflation → tilt toward ARS instruments
- When devaluation risk rises → tilt toward USD instruments
- CEDEARs provide natural USD hedge while staying in the Argentine system

### Dollar MEP Regulation Update
- BCRA Communication A 8336 (Sep 2025): 90-day cross-restriction between official market and MEP/CCL
- Buying dollars in official market blocks MEP/CCL for 90 days and vice-versa
- Dollar MEP rose 26.3% in first 10 months of 2025

### Bimonetario Decision Tree
```
Income received in ARS
├── Pay essential expenses (ARS)
├── Emergency fund → 3-6 months in Plazo Fijo / Money Market FCI
├── Short-term savings (<1 year)
│   ├── Inflation declining → LECAPs / Plazo Fijo traditional
│   └── Inflation rising → BONCER / Plazo Fijo UVA
└── Long-term investment (>1 year)
    ├── USD hedge → CEDEARs (diversified)
    ├── Income → ONs (corporate bonds, USD)
    ├── Growth → Merval equities + CEDEARs tech
    └── Store of value → Dollar MEP savings
```
